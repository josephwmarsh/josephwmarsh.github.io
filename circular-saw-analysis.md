## Project Overview
This project involved a comprehensive mechanical design audit of a 7-1/4" corded circular saw, focusing on power transmission and safety-critical components. The primary objective was to evaluate the drive shaft, driving gear, and blade retention system under operational load cycles and identify opportunities for manufacturing optimization.

### Key Engineering Skills Applied
* **Fatigue & Stress Analysis:** Evaluated the drive shaft under combined loading (bending from cutting forces and torsion from motor torque) applying both **Goodman** and **Gerber** failure criteria to determine material endurance limits.
* **FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis):** Conducted systematic risk assessments on the driving gear (tooth shear/wear), blade guard return spring (fatigue failure), and blade retention bolt (vibrational loosening).
* **Cost-Benefit Material Trade Study:** Compared 1018 and 1045 Cold Finish Carbon Steel to find the optimal balance of fatigue resistance and unit cost for the DIY market niche.

### Optimization Results
The analysis revealed that the original drive shaft was over-engineered with a safety factor of 4.27. By calculating the precise operational requirements, I proposed:
* Reducing the maximum shaft diameter from **0.5" to 0.4"**.
* This optimization maintained a safe **Factor of Safety of 2.10** while significantly reducing material volume and manufacturing costs.
* All analysis and gear geometry adhered to **AGMA standards**.

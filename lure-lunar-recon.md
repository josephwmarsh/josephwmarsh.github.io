## Project Overview
The **Lunar Underground Reconnaissance Expedition (LURE)** project addresses one of the most significant challenges for long-term lunar presence: identifying and characterizing safe, shielded environments for human habitation. Lunar lava tubes offer natural protection from extreme temperature swings, micrometeoroids, and solar radiation, but their deep, rubble-filled entrances (skylights) present a massive barrier to exploration.



### Mission & Engineering Objectives
I led the mechanical design of a robotic architecture focused on the **Marius Hills Pit**, a known 80m-deep lunar skylight. The system was engineered to bridge the gap between orbital data and in-situ characterization:
* **Deployment Mechanism:** Designed a multi-stage robotic deployment system capable of lowering a specialized sensor payload from the lunar surface to the lava tube floor. 
* **Navigation & Egress:** Engineered the mobility framework for a primary rover to navigate the steep, loose debris of the pit rim while managing tether tension and communications relay.
* **Environmental Hardening:** Selected materials and mechanical seals to withstand the high-vacuum environment and the abrasive nature of lunar regolith (moon dust), which poses a high risk of mechanical binding in moving parts.

### Technical Skills Applied
* **Aerospace Systems Design:** Balancing strict mass budgets with the mechanical redundancy required for a remote, unserviceable mission.
* **Robotics & Mobility:** Developing traversal strategies for low-gravity, high-slope environments with variable surface friction.
* **Mission Architecture:** Integrating power, thermal, and communication subsystems into a cohesive mechanical chassis.

### Project Impact
This architecture provides a viable roadmap for the "Precursor" phase of lunar settlement—gathering the ground-truth data necessary to certify underground voids for the first generation of permanent lunar data centers and astronaut refuges.
